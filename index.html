ROY TUSCANY

GIMME SOME SKIN

High Fives founder Roy Tuscany dedicates his life to giving back.

Even at idle, Roy Tuscany’s engine runs at a high RPM.That thirst for speed contributed to his life-altering injury in 2006, when he over-revved his takeoff on a jump at Mammoth Mountain—and overshot the landing. His meteoric fall smashed his T12 vertebra and relegated Tuscany to life as a paraplegic.

Yet 43 days later, Tuscany walked out of the Reno hospital. Luck, surgeons, rehab, and Tuscany’s positive attitude revived the battered connection between his spine and legs. Now 36, Tuscany has channeled his upbeat energy into the High Fives Foundation since 2009. The Truckee-based nonprofit and its Empowerment Fund helps injured athletes pay for living expenses,adaptive equipment, and medical care. High Fives operates out of the CR Johnson Healing Center, which provides Truckee area residents and High Fives athletes with a 2,800 square-foot training facility, physical therapy, and a host of alternative medicines (such as acupuncture and massage) that many insurers won’t cover. In its prevention role,High Fives also conducts snow-safety programs in local schools to help kids make better decisions. Its latest educational video highlights the critical mistakes made by five High Fives athletes.

All that good work doesn’t come free, and as a nonprofit, High Fives relies on donations. “Squaw Valley Alpine Meadows is our largest corporate partner,” says Tuscany. “Their contributions sustain everything we do, and help spread our message among their partners.”(Toyota and GoPro—both Squaw Alpine resort partners—also support High Fives.) But, says Tuscany, the resort is the easiest to work with. “Andy Wirth[Squaw Alpine’s President and COO] called me up one day and said, “I see you have this tournament coming up. Do you need a title sponsor?’ That’s… really … awesome.” Disseminating stoke is also part of High Fives’ mission, and Tuscany’s spirit turns fundraisers into riotous throwdowns—especially the annual Squaw Valley Prom on New Year’s Eve in the Olympic Valley Lodge.“It’s is the best party in all the land,” brags Tuscany.
